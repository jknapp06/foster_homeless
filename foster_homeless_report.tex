\section{Technical Assistance and Supports Serving Students Experiencing
Homelessness and Foster Youth in Fairfield-Suisun
Unified}\label{technical-assistance-and-supports-serving-students-experiencing-homelessness-and-foster-youth-in-fairfield-suisun-unified}

This report was prepared by the Assessment Research and Evaluation team
from the Solano County Office of Education to share with local LEAs
options for supporting their students -- especially those experiencing
homelessness and foster youth. The 2022-23 census day enrollment for
Fairfield-Suisun Unified was 70 foster youth and 250 students reported
as experiencing homelessness. The cumulative enrollment for students
experiencing homelessness was 473. These totals are greater in most
cases than the number of students needed to show improvements across
several student groups.

The 2023 California School Dashboard (Dashboard), released on December
15, 2023, reflects a full return of California's accountability system
with the reporting of Status (current year data), Change (the difference
from prior year data), and Performance Levels (colors) for all state
indicators except the College/Career Readiness indicator. The analysis
in this report includes dashboard indicators where foster youth and
students experiencing homelessness were assigned a color of red (the
lowest performance level). Then, for all student groups that were red
for those indicators, we calculated the improvement it would take for
each group to move from red to orange in that indicator if the number of
students stays the same. That analysis is summarized in the table below.

\textbf{How to read the table:} The homeless youth student group has a
color of red for the following indicators: suspension. The foster youth
student group has a color of red for the following indicators: ELA,
Math, and absenteeism. Any other student groups that were red for these
indicators were included in the table above to show student groups that
may benefit by providing additional support to foster youth and students
experiencing homelessness in Fairfield-Suisun Unified.

479 students from the Homeless Youth group were included in the
calculation -- the denominator. The current status is 11.9\%. The final
column indicates the change in numerator that would lead to a
performance level of orange on next year's dashboard if the number of
students enrolled remains the same. \textbf{This means, if 2 fewer
students} are suspended, the \textbf{Homeless Youth} student group would
be orange on next year's dashboard for \textbf{suspension}.
Additionally:

\begin{itemize}
\item
  If \textbf{1 more students} meet the standard on the CAASPP ELA exam,
  the \textbf{Foster Youth} student group would be orange on next year's
  dashboard for \textbf{ELA}.
\item
  If \textbf{1 more students} meet the standard on the CAASPP math exam,
  the \textbf{Foster Youth} student group would be orange on next year's
  dashboard for \textbf{Math}.
\item
  If \textbf{1 fewer students} are chronically absent, the
  \textbf{Foster Youth} student group would be orange on next year's
  dashboard for \textbf{absenteeism}.

  If \textbf{5 fewer students} are chronically absent, the
  \textbf{Asian} student group would be orange on next year's dashboard
  for \textbf{absenteeism}.

  If \textbf{37 more students} meet the standard on the CAASPP math
  exam, the \textbf{Black/African American} student group would be

  American} student group would be orange on next year's dashboard for

  If \textbf{10 fewer students} are suspended, the \textbf{Students with

\end{itemize}

Analysis into the outcome data for foster youth and students
experiencing homelessness at Fairfield-Suisun Unified suggests that
supporting these vulnerable populations may also improve the standing of
other groups. Differentiated Assistance eligibility presents an
opportunity for individually designed assistance, to address identified
performance issues, including significant disparities in performance
among student groups\footnote{https://www.cde.ca.gov/sp/sw/t1/csss.asp}.

\subsection{Student Support Resources in Solano
County}\label{student-support-resources-in-solano-county}

\textbf{Restorative Practices (alternative to suspension)} Restorative
Practices (RP) are grounded in traditional practices of indigenous
communities from around the world that underscore the value of respect,
compassion, dignity, and inclusion of all members of the community. RP
promotes positive culture by strengthening the quality of relationships
across the school community. It includes interventions when harm has
occurred, as well as practices that prevent harm by building a sense of
belonging, safety, and shared social responsibility throughout the
school community. Conflict and behavior challenges are viewed through
the lens of restorative justice philosophy; one that brings all parties
affected by an incident together in an inclusive process to discover the
root cause and impact of the behavior, with the intention to repair
relationships and restore the community. If you would like your
school/district to be trained or have any other questions, please reach
out to Kerri Berkowitz at
\href{mailto:kberkowitz@solanocoe.net}{\nolinkurl{kberkowitz@solanocoe.net}}.

\textbf{Neurosequential Model in Education (NME) Pilot Program} The
Neurosequential Model in Education (NME) draws upon the NMT (a
neurodevelopmentally-informed, biologically respectful perspective on
human development and functioning) to help educators understand student
behavior and performance. The goals of NME are to educate faculty and
students in basic concepts of neurosequential development and then teach
them how to apply this knowledge to the teaching and learning process.
NME is not a specific ``intervention;'' it is a way to educate school
staff about brain development and developmental trauma and then to
further teach them how to apply that knowledge to their work with
students in and outside the classroom, particularly those students with
adverse childhood experiences. Solano County Office of Education has
been recently accepted into the 2024 NME Pilot Program with the
California Department of Education -Foster Youth Education Unit. To
learn more about the pilot program, please contact Akon M. Walker at
\href{mailto:awalker@solanocoe.net}{\nolinkurl{awalker@solanocoe.net}}.

\textbf{Positive Behavioral Intervention and Supports} Positive
Behavioral Interventions and Supports (PBIS) is a multi-tiered
instructional behavior framework. It is grounded in a continuum of
evidence-based practices that are used consistently school-wide, for all
students by all staff (certificated, classified, and management). When
schoolwide interventions and strategies are implemented with fidelity,
unwanted behaviors are prevented, pro-social skills are taught, and new
skills are reinforced. SCOE provides PBIS training, coaching, and
support in a variety of flexible formats. If you would like your
school/district to be trained or have other questions, please reach out
to Dr.~Jen Buzolich at

or Shomonique Johnson at
