\section{Technical Assistance and Supports Serving Students Experiencing
Homelessness in Dixon
Unified}\label{technical-assistance-and-supports-serving-students-experiencing-homelessness-in-dixon-unified}

The 2023 California School Dashboard (Dashboard), released on December
15, 2023, reflects a full return of California's accountability system
with the reporting of Status (current year data), Change (the difference
from prior year data), and Performance Levels (colors) for all state
indicators except the College/Career Readiness indicator.

In the 2022-23 school year, there were 210 students reported as
experiencing homelessness in Dixon Unified during the academic year.
Beginning with dashboard indicators where students experiencing
homelessness were assigned a color of red (the lowest performance
level), we looked at other student groups with red in that indicator. We
then calculated how many students it would take for each group to move
from red to orange. That analysis is summarized in the table below.

\textbf{How to read the table:} The Black/African American student group
has a color of red in the Math indicator. The homeless youth student
group is also red for this indicator. 38 students from the Black/African
American group were included in the calculation -- the denominator. The
current status is -124.9. The final column indicates the number of
students that would need to change to earn a color of orange on next
year's dashboard if the number of students enrolled remains the same.
\textbf{This means, if 1 more students} meet the standard on the CAASPP
math exam, the \textbf{Black/African American} student group would be
orange on next year's dashboard for \textbf{Math}. Additionally:

\begin{itemize}
\item
  If \textbf{3 more students} meet the standard on the CAASPP math exam,
  the \textbf{Homeless Youth} student group would be orange on next
  year's dashboard for \textbf{Math}.
\item
  If \textbf{1 fewer students} are suspended, the \textbf{Homeless
  Youth} student group would be orange on next year's dashboard for
  \textbf{suspension}.
\item
  If \textbf{2 fewer students} are suspended, the \textbf{Students with
  Disabilities} student group would be orange on next year's dashboard
  for \textbf{suspension}.
\end{itemize}

Analysis into the outcome data for homeless youth at Dixon Unified
reflects that supporting these vulnerable populations may also improve
the standing of other groups.

\subsection{Student Support Resources in Solano County for Targeted
Group}\label{student-support-resources-in-solano-county-for-targeted-group}

\textbf{Restorative Practices (alternative to suspension)} Restorative
Practices (RP) are grounded in traditional practices of indigenous
communities from around the world that underscore the value of respect,
compassion, dignity, and inclusion of all members of the community. RP
promotes positive culture by strengthening the quality of relationships
across the school community. It includes interventions when harm has
occurred, as well as practices that prevent harm by building a sense of
belonging, safety, and shared social responsibility throughout the
school community. Conflict and behavior challenges are viewed through
the lens of restorative justice philosophy; one that brings all parties
affected by an incident together in an inclusive process to discover the
root cause and impact of the behavior, with the intention to repair
relationships and restore the community. If you would like your
school/district to be trained or have any other questions, please reach
out to Kerri Berkowitz at
\href{mailto:kberkowitz@solanocoe.net}{\nolinkurl{kberkowitz@solanocoe.net}}.

\textbf{Neurosequential Model in Education (NME) Pilot Program} The
Neurosequential Model in Education (NME) draws upon the NMT (a
neurodevelopmentally-informed, biologically respectful perspective on
human development and functioning) to help educators understand student
behavior and performance. The goals of NME are to educate faculty and
students in basic concepts of neurosequential development and then teach
them how to apply this knowledge to the teaching and learning process.
NME is not a specific ``intervention;'' it is a way to educate school
staff about brain development and developmental trauma and then to
further teach them how to apply that knowledge to their work with
students in and outside the classroom, particularly those students with
adverse childhood experiences. Solano County Office of Education has
been recently accepted into the 2024 NME Pilot Program with the
California Department of Education -Foster Youth Education Unit. To
learn more about the pilot program, please contact Akon M. Walker at
\href{mailto:awalker@solanocoe.net}{\nolinkurl{awalker@solanocoe.net}}.

\textbf{Positive Behavioral Intervention and Supports} Positive
Behavioral Interventions and Supports (PBIS) is a multi-tiered
instructional behavior framework. It is grounded in a continuum of
evidence-based practices that are used consistently school-wide, for all
students by all staff (certificated, classified, and management). When
schoolwide interventions and strategies are implemented with fidelity,
unwanted behaviors are prevented, pro-social skills are taught, and new
skills are reinforced. SCOE provides PBIS training, coaching, and
support in a variety of flexible formats. If you would like your
school/district to be trained or have other questions, please reach out
to Dr.~Jen Buzolich at
\href{mailto:jbuzolich@solanocoe.net}{\nolinkurl{jbuzolich@solanocoe.net}}
or Shomonique Johnson at
\href{mailto:sjohnson@solanocoe.net}{\nolinkurl{sjohnson@solanocoe.net}}.
